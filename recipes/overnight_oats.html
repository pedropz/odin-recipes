Overnight Oats

Description

Overnight Oats is a quick, simple, versatile and nutritious breakfast option. If you use natural ingredients it can be a very healthy
choice and oats are a natural source of dietary fiber.

It's also a refreshing meal on hotter days!

Ingredients:

  • 300ml of any milk - preferably one with high protein like soy milk
  • one glass and a half of oats - preferably fine or ultrafine
  • one banana
  • some berries of your choosing - I personally opt for dried blueberries or fresh strawberries
  • chia seeds - they have a nice texture when soaked and are a great source of omega 3's
  • 20g of peanut butter - the more peanut butter the tastier your overnight oats will be, but I'd caution against using too
    much as peanut butter is very calorie dense and high in saturated fats
  • any other type of fruit you may want to add is fine. :)

Steps

Now, there's no right way to make Overnight Oats. If you wish, you mays just throw all your ingredients in a glass jar and mix them.
I usually have it layered as it avoids some annoying clumps of oats. It's up to you if you'd rather do it layered or mixed. Keep
reading if you'd like to know how I recommend you layer it.

  1. First you'll want to get a glass of your milk of choice and your scoop of peanut butter and mix them with a blender or similar
    equipment. This step is optional but if you don't do it you risk having dry-ish lumps of peanut butter and oats waiting for you.
  2. Now actually onto layering it. Find a tall glass jar (that's cup-ish) of reasonable size. Grab your fruits. Slice those fruits,
    if applicable and place (or throw if you're feeling particularly violent) them into the bottom of the jar. This step is important
    because it makes it unlikely that you'll get to the end of your meal and find some sad, plain oats staring back at you.
  3. Gently pour enough milk to cover your fruits.
  4. Pour some chia seeds.
  5. If you want more peanut butter, this would be a good time to throw some in. Other wise, just go ahead and pour some oats in.
    Be careful to not pour too many oats at once, as that can make it so the milk cannot reach the bottom, leaving you with some dry,
    boring oats.
  6. Pour some more milk in. If you notice that the milk isn't reaching the bottom of your oats, try gently shaking the jar.
    If that doesn't work move the oats around with a spoon and make a mental note to pour less oats next time.
  7. Another optional scoop of peanut butter.
  8. Keep repeating this process until you've either used all of your ingredients or all the space in the jar.
  9. Enjoy! :)
